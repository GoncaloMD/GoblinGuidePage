Intro to Burn

If you're looking to get started with Burn in Modern, then this is the page for you! Hopefully, this is enough to get you started.

"Isn't Burn just an easy intro deck?"

Short answer: no. One of the biggest misconceptions of this deck is that it's super easy to play, and that it's a good choice to lend to your friend that's new to the game. This simply isn't true. While Burn has quite a simple plan, a very linear one to boot, give it to someone that's still not sure about what the difference between Instants and Sorceries is, and they're guaranteed to have a bad time. Modern just isn't a format for beginners. It's a cutthroat one where mistakes are weighed in tons, and Burn is a deck that wins by grams. One wrong decision in the many that the deck presents will lose you games.

Having said that, general modern knowledge and a bit of understanding in what makes this deck tick goes a long way in making it a very quick pick up. Mastering it (something which I don't claim to have done) is another story, and requires you to be able to look at the game in a more mathematical way. But we'll talk about that some other time. This page will cover the basics, the "what makes the deck tick" I mentioned earlier.

What is Burn all about?

Burn is all about direct damage. The name "Burn" comes exactly from that effect, i.e., cards that can directly damage opponents. Effectively, variations of Lightning Bolt .

What cards does Burn play?

In this section I'm gonna go over the mainboard cards that Burn plays, at least the core, established ones. Alternative or more "avant-garde" will be covered somewhere else.

Fixed slots

  • Goblin Guide: the best creature in our deck. Some people underestimate it because they read it and think it'll draw their opponents a ton of cards, including helping them curve out their mana. But that's not how the card works. Because Goblin Guide draws lands on reveal, a land which they would've drawn either way, it actually helps your opponents find non-land spells. That means that, barring a deck with an unusually high number of lands, it will just give you info about your opponents hand around 66% percent of the time. That's a pretty good deal, when you add to it that Burn results in fast games, where 1 or 2 extra cards are unlikely to affect the game. Because of this, I don't read Goblin Guide as a liability, but as a strength. I don't think that I then have to explain why a hasty 2/2 for R is a good card to be playing, but I will anyway. It comes out swinging, and 2 damage per turn add up quick. It puts us in a position where our opponents need to answer it soon, or we just get too much damage for that one Red mana and become veeeery unlikely to lose.
